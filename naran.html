In a Glimpse:


Naran is a picturesque town located in the Kaghan Valley, part of the Khyber Pakhtunkhwa province in Pakistan. Surrounded by the majestic Himalayan and Karakoram mountain ranges, Naran is a paradise for nature lovers and adventure seekers. The town sits at an elevation of approximately 8,202 feet (2,500 meters) above sea level, providing a cool and refreshing climate throughout the year. Visitors are captivated by the serene lakes, lush green meadows, and the overall tranquility that Naran offers.
The Kaghan Valley, where Naran is situated, is dotted with charming villages, making it an ideal destination for those seeking a peaceful retreat amidst the stunning beauty of nature.

The snowfall in winter adds to the enchanting beauty of the landscape, attracting winter sports enthusiasts and those seeking a snowy adventure.



Places to Visit



Lake Saif-ul-Mulook:


Lake Saif-ul-Mulook, often referred to as the "Lake of Fairies," is a stunning alpine lake surrounded by snow-capped peaks. The lake is known for its crystal-clear turquoise-blue water, reflecting the surrounding mountains. Visitors can enjoy boating in the lake, taking in the breathtaking views of the landscape. The lake is also a hub for various migratory birds, adding to its natural charm.



Ansoo Lake:


Ansoo Lake, named for its tear-like shape, is a hidden gem in the Himalayas. This high-altitude lake requires a challenging trek to reach, making it a favorite among adventure enthusiasts. The panoramic views from Ansoo Lake include snow-covered peaks and lush green valleys, creating a mesmerizing backdrop for those who undertake the trek.



Babusar Top:


Babusar Top is a breathtaking mountain pass situated at an elevation of over 13,600 feet (4,100 meters) above sea level. The journey to Babusar Top offers panoramic views of the Kaghan Valley and surrounding mountains. The pass is accessible during the summer months when the road is clear of snow, providing travelers with an opportunity to witness the natural beauty of the region.



Saif-ul-Mulook National Park:


The national park surrounding Lake Saif-ul-Mulook is a sanctuary for biodiversity. The park is home to a variety of flora and fauna, including different species of birds and mammals. Nature enthusiasts can explore the park's trails, witnessing the beauty of the Himalayan ecosystem. The pristine environment and diverse wildlife make it an ideal destination for eco-tourism.



Where to Stay:


Naran offers a range of accommodation options catering to various preferences and budgets. From cozy guesthouses and comfortable hotels to camping sites amidst nature, visitors can choose accommodation that suits their needs. Many establishments provide stunning views of the surrounding mountains, enhancing the overall experience of staying in this picturesque town.



What to Buy:


The local markets in Naran are a treasure trove of handmade crafts and traditional items. Visitors can purchase intricately woven woolen products, embroidered shawls, and local jewelry as souvenirs. Additionally, the market offers unique items such as carved walking sticks and leather goods. Exploring the markets provides an opportunity to engage with the local culture and take home a piece of the Kaghan Valley.



Transport Modes


Naran is accessible by road from major cities, including Islamabad and Lahore. The journey to Naran is an adventure in itself, as the road winds through scenic landscapes and picturesque villages. Travelers can opt for buses, private cars, or shared vans to reach Naran. The journey is not only a means of transportation but also an immersive experience, offering glimpses of the natural beauty that awaits in this Himalayan haven.
